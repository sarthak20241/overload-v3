# Brag Plan: Overload

## What is this app?
Overload is a gamified strength-training tracker: you log your lifts and it turns
progressive overload into a game — counting volume, catching PRs, tracking your
streak, leveling you up with XP, and giving you an AI coach ("Coach Drona") that
knows every rep you've ever done.

## The angle
The name *is* the pitch. "Progressive overload" is the one law of getting
stronger — add a little every week. Overload makes that law visible: every set
you log makes a number go *up*. Volume up. PR badges. Streak up. XP bar fills,
and you level up from Beginner → Athlete → Legend. It's a lifting app that feels
like a game, dressed in a sharp lime-on-black identity. The video should feel
like a clean, confident app-store launch where every beat is a number climbing.

## Hook (first 2-3 seconds)
Pure black (#0a0a0a). The wordmark **OVERLOAD** slams in, heavy. A lime
(#c8ff00) XP bar sweeps to full beneath it and a small lime **LV 12** chip pops.
One line settles under it: **"Every rep counts."** The hook is the brand + the
core promise in one motion: the bar filling is the whole product in 2 seconds.

## Key moments (the middle)
- **The real dashboard** — phone frame, dark theme: "Good morning / Athlete",
  the lime XP/level bar, the weekly calendar with lime workout dots, the
  **VOLUME** stat card with its blue area chart, the **MUSCLES** donut. Cards
  arrive one by one.
- **Coach Drona** — the purple→blue AI hero card: "Coach Drona" + a **NEW**
  badge, the line **"Your coach. Knows every rep, every PR."**, and the chip row
  (Chat · Quick Workout · Full Plan). Slides up clean.
- **The overload payoff** — a "Push Day" workout row appears with a lime
  **PR ×2** award badge and a green **+45kg ↑** volume delta. A big lime number
  ticks up. This is the name cashing out: you overloaded, and the app caught it.

## Outro / punchline
The XP bar fills the last stretch and snaps to full — **LEVEL UP** flashes lime,
the tier reads **ATHLETE 🏆**. Resolve to the **OVERLOAD** wordmark with the
tagline **"Lift. Level up. Repeat."** A single lime accent rings out over the
music fade.

## User flow worth showing
The real loop of *using* Overload: **open the dashboard (level + week at a
glance) → the app catches your PR and volume gain on a logged workout → your XP
bar fills and you level up.** Entry → key result → reward. The centerpiece
scenes (2 and 4) recreate the actual app screens — dashboard and the recent-
workout PR row — not a marketing diagram.

## Tone
- Preset: app-store
- Creative direction: "gym-app launch — lime-on-black, every rep levels you up"
- Interpretation: clean feature-card reveals, smooth slides, confident pacing;
  energetic (it's a gym app) but never messy. Numbers climbing carry the energy,
  not flashing text. Restraint on chrome; let the lime accent and real UI do the
  talking.

## Audio direction
- Role: warm rhythmic bed with clean, motion-matched accents (app-store polish)
- Music: `happy-beats-business-moves-vol-11-by-ende-dot-app.mp3` — upbeat,
  modern, ~114.84 BPM
- Music treatment: start at 0; sit at a confident level under the UI; gentle
  fade under the final wordmark/logo so the closing lime accent rings out.
- Music cue guidance: bundled preset
  (`assets/music/happy-beats-business-moves-vol-11-by-ende-dot-app.music-cues.json`),
  ~114.84 BPM. Strong cues at **1.60, 3.70, 5.80, 8.96, 12.65, 17.91, 22.65s**.
  Target the hook/bar-fill near 1.60s, the Coach card near 8.96s, the PR payoff
  near 12.65s, and the LEVEL UP near 17.91s. Beat grid ~0.52s apart for the
  one-by-one card reveals.
- Audio-reactive treatment: subtle — let the lime glow / XP-bar fill presence
  breathe with the music's energy. No waveform bars, no equalizer.
- SFX posture: moderate, motion-matched — a soft tick as the XP bar fills, light
  card sounds for one-by-one reveals, a short bright announcement cue on the PR
  badge, one clean accent on LEVEL UP. Professional restraint.
- Audio-coupled moments: XP bar fill (rising tick), stat cards arriving one by
  one (card sounds on the beat grid), PR badge (announcement), counter tick-up,
  LEVEL UP (accent), final wordmark (let accent ring over the fade).
- Restraint rule: no strobing, no dense SFX layering, no sound on every beat —
  accents land on the planned strong cues only.
