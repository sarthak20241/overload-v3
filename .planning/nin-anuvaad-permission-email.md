# Data permission requests (diet tracking)

Two letters to send. Goal: written permission to use authoritative Indian nutrition
data in a commercial app. If granted, we use IFCT/INDB directly and Indian coverage
jumps; if not, the "compute, don't copy" path still ships. This has the longest lead
time in the plan, so send early. Fill the [bracketed] bits before sending.

---

## 1. ICMR-NIN — IFCT 2017 (Indian Food Composition Tables)

**To:** director-nin@icmr.gov.in (Director, ICMR-NIN) — also cc the IFCT/data contact if
listed at nin.res.in. Verify the current address on the NIN site before sending.
**Subject:** Permission request: use of IFCT 2017 data in a fitness app (commercial)

Respected Sir/Madam,

I am [Full Name], developer of Overload, a fitness and nutrition tracking app aimed at
Indian users. I am writing to request written permission to use the food composition
data from the Indian Food Composition Tables (IFCT 2017) within the app.

I note that the IFCT 2017 copyright terms permit reproduction for personal use with
acknowledgment, but require prior written permission to store or reproduce the data in
electronic format for creating a product. My use is exactly that case: I would like to
include IFCT raw-food nutrient values in the app's food database so Indian users can log
meals accurately against authoritative national data.

Specifically I am requesting permission to:
- store IFCT 2017 nutrient values in the app's database and ship them to end users for
  offline food logging, and
- display per-food macro values (energy, protein, carbohydrate, fat) to users.

I will prominently attribute ICMR-NIN and IFCT 2017 as the data source within the app,
and I am happy to comply with any conditions, attribution format, licensing fee, or data-
use agreement you require. Please let me know the process, terms, and any cost.

Thank you for your work in making IFCT publicly available; it is the gold standard for
Indian food data and I would prefer to use it correctly and with your blessing.

Respectfully,
[Full Name]
[Email] · [Phone] · [App name / website]

## 2. Anuvaad Solutions / Dr. Lindsay Jaacks — INDB (Indian Nutrient Databank)

**To:** the contact listed on the INDB GitHub repo / the Curr Dev Nutr 2024 paper
(corresponding author Dr. Lindsay Jaacks, University of Edinburgh) and Anuvaad Solutions.
Verify current addresses before sending.
**Subject:** Permission request: use of the Indian Nutrient Databank (INDB) in a commercial app

Dear Dr. Jaacks and the Anuvaad team,

I am [Full Name], developer of Overload, a fitness and nutrition tracking app for Indian
users. I came across the Indian Nutrient Databank (INDB) and its 2024 publication, and the
1,014 cooked Indian recipes with per-serving macros are exactly the kind of authoritative,
dish-level Indian data that no openly-licensed Western database provides.

The repository describes INDB as open-access, but I did not find an explicit data license
permitting commercial redistribution, and I understand INDB is built on IFCT 2017. Before
using any of it, I want to ask directly:

- May I include INDB-derived per-dish macro values in a commercial app's food database
  (stored and shipped to users for offline logging)?
- If so, what attribution and terms would you like, and are there any restrictions given
  the IFCT 2017 inputs?

I will attribute INDB and the publication prominently, and I am glad to follow any
conditions you set. If direct use is not possible, I would still value your guidance on
referencing INDB's recipe compositions to compute our own values from openly-licensed
ingredient sources.

Thank you for building and sharing INDB. It is a real gap-filler for Indian nutrition data.

Best regards,
[Full Name]
[Email] · [Phone] · [App name / website]
